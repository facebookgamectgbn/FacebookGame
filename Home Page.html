Feature

Criterion Games considered developing a new game multiple times, potentially a Burnout Versus RR,[5][6] but nothing has come of this; they have also expressed a desire to move away from racing games in particular.[7] Dan Geisler, main programmer and co-designer of the Sega Genesis trilogy, was working on a new title along with a number of the original Road Rash staff members, then named Hard Rider: Back in the Saddle; he first announced it via a Reddit thread,[8] and frequently mentioned progress on his Twitter account.[9] However, he was unable to find funding for it and dropped the idea.



Music

The Sega Genesis trilogy features music by EA composers Rob Hubbard (1 and II), Michael Bartlow (1), Tony Berkeley (II), and Don Veca (II and 3).[3] Later entries were among the first video games to include licensed music tracks from major recording artists in gameplay.[4]
